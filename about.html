A Best Friend
11 years old · 2009

I met someone who steered the direction of my life for the next decade. He was 2 years older than me, so I thought I could learn a lot from hanging around with him.

We called each other everyday when we got flip phones and did everything together since we enrolled in the same high school and college later in life. Hanging out with older kids became normal to me as all his friends became mine and they still are to this day.

My First Business
12-13 years old · 2010-2012

Before middle school, I made a lot of pennies playing games and taking online surveys. However, these were the years when I made my first hundreds of dollars.

Though I never thought of myself as a good student, I was good enough to stay in the top class, do homework for other students in exchange for money, and get into a prestigious high school where I would unknowingly have some of the best years of my life.

Connie
14 years old · 2012

The scariest teacher I ever had taught me what it meant to be punctual and strive for perfection in work.

In her architecture class, we would design structures both by hand and digitally where the slightest mistake would give us a failing grade. I vividly remember the one day I was late to her class by 3 minutes.

She scolded me and I'm grateful for that because that made all my years after better.
